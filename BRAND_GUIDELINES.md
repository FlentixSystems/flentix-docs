6. DESIGN PRINCIPLES
Core Design Philosophy
Flentix Systems design is built on four foundational principles: clarity, simplicity, professionalism, and
scaleability. Every design decision should advance one or more of these principles.
1. Clean & Minimal
Remove unnecessary visual noise. Every element should have a purpose. Clear interfaces make
professional tools feel accessible.
Application:
- Eliminate decorative elements
- Use whitespace to provide visual breathing room
- Group related information thoughtfully
- One primary action per screen when possible
2. Geometric Precision
Our brand uses geometric forms (the portal frame) consistently. Design systems should reflect this
precision.
Application:
- Align all elements to the grid
- Use geometric shapes in iconography
- Sharp, defined corners (not rounded for softness)
- Consistent 90-degree angles in layouts
3. Purposeful Whitespace
Whitespace is an active design element, not empty space. It guides attention, improves readability,
and conveys quality.
Application:
- Spacing scale: 4px, 8px, 16px, 24px, 32px, 48px, 64px
- Use consistent spacing around elements
- Group related content; separate unrelated content
- Breathing room around CTAs and important features
4. Hierarchy First
Visual hierarchy guides the eye and communicates importance. Flentix interfaces prioritize clarity
through clear hierarchy.
Application:
- Largest type = most important content
- Use color sparingly to highlight key elements
- Typography weight carries more weight than size
- Logical reading order (top to bottom, left to right)

7. TONE OF VOICE
Voice & Personality
Flentix Systems tone of voice reflects our brand personality: professional but not corporate,
knowledgeable but humble, supportive and human-centered.
Core Voice Characteristics
Professional but Not Corporate
We’re credible, reliable, and expert without being stiff, jargon-heavy, or corporate-sounding. We use
clear language and avoid buzzwords.
 Example (Good):
“Set up your booking system in 15 minutes”
 Example (Avoid):
“Leverage our enterprise-grade infrastructure to optimize operational workflows”
• 
• 
• 
• 
15
Supportive & Empathetic
We understand the challenges of running an independent workspace business. We’re not lecturing—
we’re helping. We acknowledge pain points and position ourselves as partners.
 Example (Good):
“We know managing bookings manually is exhausting. Let’s fix that.”
 Example (Avoid):
“Inefficient processes compromise operational excellence.”
Clear & Straightforward
We explain what we do and how it helps in plain language. No obfuscation, no fluff. Value comes first.
 Example (Good):
“Smart lock integration means guests get access codes automatically”
 Example (Avoid):
“Synergistic IoT-enabled access control paradigms”
Confident but Humble
We’re excellent at what we do, but we don’t oversell or make unrealistic promises. We acknowledge
that we serve a specific market and we’re optimized for that.
 Example (Good):
“Professional booking system built for growth”
 Example (Avoid):
“Revolutionary AI-powered platform disrupting the industry”
Writing Guidelines & Standards
Capitalization
Brand/Product Names: Flentix Systems (always capitalized)
Feature Names: Sentence case (“smart lock integration” not “Smart Lock Integration”)
CTAs & Buttons: Title Case (“Get Started” not “get started”)
Proper Nouns: Standard rules apply
Contractions
Use contractions freely (we’re, you’ll, it’s) for a more conversational, approachable tone. Contractions
are not informal—they’re more human.
 “We’re here to help”
 “We are here to help”
Perspective & Pronouns
Use second person (you, your) to address operators directly
Use first person (we, our) for Flentix’s perspective

Avoid third person (one, they) which creates distance
Grammar & Syntax
Active voice over passive: “You save 5 hours per week” not “5 hours per week are saved”
Short paragraphs: 2-3 sentences maximum for body copy
Bullet points: Use for scannability and lists
Vary sentence length: Mix short and longer sentences for rhythm
Spanish Market Considerations
For Spanish materials, adapt the following:
Units: Use metric (sqm, kg, etc. not sqft, lbs)
Date Format: DD/MM/YYYY (not MM/DD/YYYY)
Time Format: 24-hour format (15:30 not 3:30 PM)
Currency: Euro symbol (€50 or 50€, both acceptable in Spain)
Language: Spanish translations should maintain tone—professional but warm
